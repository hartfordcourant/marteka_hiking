The Branford Trail is a 28-mile perimeter trail around the shoreline town connecting town parks and land trust preserves. During my travels across the state, I've never seen it duplicated. And then I traveled to Groton and climbed aboard the Cross Town Trail — a miniature version of Branford's path.

The six-mile trail connects the waters of the Poquonnock River to Beebe Cove on Long Island Sound. In between, Cross Town connects two state parks, three land trust properties and a town park and preserve.

The best part about it? It remains in the coastal forest for most of the journey.

Since I've written about both Bluff Point State Park and Haley Farm State Park in the past, I started my journey at the 88-acre Mortimer Wright Preserve along Groton Long Point Road. Although there are trails winding throughout the parcel, Cross Town — marked with bright blue blazes on trees — runs along the preserve's western boundary before linking up with the 75-acre Merritt Family Forest protected by the Groton Open Space Association.
